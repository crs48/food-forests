Where there is water, some frameworks add a ninth layer: the aquatic and
wetland zone. A pond or a boggy edge multiplies the food forest's diversity,
offering habitat for frogs and dragonflies, storing water, and growing crops no
dry-land layer can.

The number of layers — seven, eight, or nine — is a design convention rather
than a rule. Hart named seven; fungi and water are welcome additions wherever
your site invites them.
