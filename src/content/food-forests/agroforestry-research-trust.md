Martin Crawford is one of the world's leading authorities on temperate forest
gardening. He founded the Agroforestry Research Trust in 1992 and, beside
Schumacher College at Dartington, planted a now-famous two-acre forest garden
that has become a living textbook for the temperate world.

His book *Creating a Forest Garden* and the Trust's plants, seeds, and courses
have shaped countless projects. (A community campaign has recently rallied to
protect the Dartington garden's future — worth checking its current status
before visiting.)
