Many designers add an eighth layer that Robert Hart's original seven left
implicit: the fungal network threading through the soil. Mycorrhizal fungi
partner with plant roots, trading water and minerals for sugars and linking the
whole forest into one cooperative web.

Nurtured with wood chips and left undisturbed, this layer can also yield edible
mushrooms — wine caps in the mulch, shiitake on inoculated logs — while quietly
building the soil that everything else depends on.
