At around 7.1 acres, Browns Mill is the largest free public food forest in the
country — a former farm four miles south of downtown Atlanta, replanted with
more than 2,500 edible and medicinal plants. It was developed by the City of
Atlanta with the Conservation Fund, Trees Atlanta, and the U.S. Forest Service.

Its purpose is squarely about people: the surrounding neighbourhood had limited
access to fresh food, and the forest was designed to help close that gap while
building community around growing and harvesting together.
