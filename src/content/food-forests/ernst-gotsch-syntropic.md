Ernst Götsch, a Swiss-born researcher who has worked in Brazil since the 1980s,
is the originator of **syntropic agriculture**: succession-driven, densely
planted agroforestry that uses no external inputs and heavy pruning to
accelerate how a forest builds itself.

On degraded pasture in Bahia he restored more than 200 hectares of Atlantic
Forest, and in the process dry springs began to flow again. His plots produce
cocoa, fruit, and timber while regenerating the very ecosystem they sit in —
farming and reforestation as the same act.
