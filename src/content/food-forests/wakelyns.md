Established in 1994 by plant pathologist Professor Martin Wolfe and Ann Wolfe,
Wakelyns is a pioneering organic agroforestry farm. Long north–south rows of
trees — more than fifty apple varieties, plus pears, cherries, plums, walnuts,
and coppiced willow and hazel — alternate with alleys of population wheat,
squash, and lentils.

The design shelters crops, houses wildlife, and spreads risk across a diversity
of harvests. Now run by the next generation, Wakelyns is a working demonstration
that trees and arable farming belong together.
