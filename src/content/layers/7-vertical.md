Vines and climbers make use of the one dimension nothing else claims: the
vertical space between the layers. Trained up a sturdy tree or trellis, they
turn trunks and edges into extra growing surface.

Climbers are the connective tissue of the food forest, weaving the tiers into
a single three-dimensional community — a grape spiralling into the canopy, or
beans clambering up a support while fixing nitrogen at their feet.
