Beneath the canopy sits the low-tree or understory layer: smaller trees, often
on dwarfing rootstock, that are happy in the filtered light of the big trees.
Pawpaws and persimmons naturally grow as forest-edge understory species, so
they feel right at home here.

This layer lets you stack far more productivity into the same footprint. Where
an orchard would leave bare grass between standard trees, the food forest fills
that vertical space with a second tier of harvest.
