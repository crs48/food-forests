Non-woody plants that die back and return each season make up the herbaceous
layer — perennial vegetables, culinary and medicinal herbs, and hard-working
support plants. This is the layer that most rewards a little diversity.

Comfrey is the star here: its deep taproot mines nutrients from far below,
and its lush leaves can be chopped and dropped as a mineral-rich mulch — a
"dynamic accumulator" feeding everything around it.
