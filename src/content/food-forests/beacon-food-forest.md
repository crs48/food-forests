Born from a 2009 permaculture design course, Beacon Food Forest turned a slope
of public utility land into one of the largest and best-known public food
forests in the United States. The first trees went in the ground in 2012, and
the site has been tended by volunteers — and open to all — ever since.

The radical idea at its centre is generosity: there are no fences and no price.
Neighbours pick fruit, herbs, and greens freely, and in doing so a piece of city
infrastructure became a commons. It has inspired a wave of public food forests
across North America.
