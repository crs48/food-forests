The shrub layer is where a lot of the everyday harvest lives — soft fruit and
berries at a comfortable picking height. Many are woodland-edge plants that
fruit well in partial shade, from currants and gooseberries to elderberry.

Some shrubs pull double duty: sea buckthorn fixes nitrogen while producing
tart, vitamin-rich berries, feeding both you and the soil.
