tagline: A living mulch that never leaves soil bare
heightRange: Ankle high & spreading

exampleSpecies:
  - Strawberry
  - Creeping thyme
  - Sweet woodruff
  - White clover (nitrogen-fixing)
---

Low, spreading plants knit across the soil surface, suppressing weeds, holding
moisture, and keeping the ground cool and covered. In permaculture, bare soil
is a wound to be dressed — the ground-cover layer is the living bandage.

Choose ground covers that earn their keep: strawberries give fruit, clover
fixes nitrogen, and creeping thyme feeds pollinators while releasing scent
underfoot.
