The canopy is the overstory — the tallest, longest-lived trees that shape the
light and character of everything below. In a food forest these are usually
full-size fruit and nut trees, sometimes mixed with nitrogen-fixing trees like
black locust that feed their neighbours.

Canopy trees are a decades-long commitment, so they are placed first and
spaced generously. Their dappled shade creates the sheltered, woodland-edge
conditions that the lower layers love — and their falling leaves become the
mulch that feeds the whole system.
