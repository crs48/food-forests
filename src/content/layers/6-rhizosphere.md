The rhizosphere is the underground layer — root crops and tubers that use the
soil itself as growing space, slotting in below plants that reach for the sky.
Because they occupy a different zone, they rarely compete with the layers above.

Roots also do quiet structural work: deep taproots break up compaction, open
channels for water and air, and, when left in place, feed the soil food web as
they decompose.
