Geoff Lawton took his design certificate with Bill Mollison in 1983 and has
since become one of permaculture's best-known teachers. At Zaytuna Farm in New
South Wales he runs the Permaculture Research Institute, training designers from
around the world.

He is most famous for the *Greening the Desert* projects in Jordan's arid Dead
Sea valley, where swales, water harvesting, and heavy mulching coaxed a green,
productive system out of salty, sun-baked ground — a vivid proof of what
regenerative design can do on the hardest land.
